# Genfy Landing Strategy Theory

## Objective

Define the theoretical direction for a stronger Genfy landing page before any implementation work. This document is intentionally strategic and conceptual. It should guide future visual exploration, Playwright review, copywriting, and structure decisions without locking the team into specific components or code changes.

## Context

Genfy already has a clear product core:

- turn products into ready-to-publish AI videos
- support TikTok Shop workflows
- help users discover, validate, and act on winning products
- extend beyond generation into a broader commerce and creator loop

The current risk is not lack of features. The risk is that the landing page presents too many product surfaces too early, which weakens clarity and onboarding.

The next version should feel more strategic, more demonstrative, and more conversion-oriented, while preserving the current Genfy essence.

## Strategic Thesis

The landing page should position Genfy as:

`the platform that helps sellers and creators find promising products and turn them into videos ready to sell`

This is stronger than presenting Genfy as only an AI video tool.

It protects the current differentiation:

- product discovery
- product validation
- video generation
- TikTok Shop relevance

It also creates a cleaner story than listing tools, engines, social, updates, and community as separate primary pillars.

## Core Positioning Shift

The homepage should move from a feature-led message to a workflow-led message.

Current style:

- here are our tools
- here are our engines
- here are our updates
- here is our community

Target style:

- here is the outcome
- here is how Genfy gets you there
- here are examples of what that looks like
- here is why Genfy is better than doing this manually
- here is the fastest way to start

## What Must Stay

The redesign should preserve these Genfy traits:

- focus on TikTok Shop and product-led commerce
- emphasis on video output, not generic AI creativity
- discovery and validation as real differentiators
- premium but practical product feel
- bilingual clarity for Portuguese and English users

The redesign should not turn Genfy into a generic “AI marketing platform” or a generic “video generator.”

## What Should Change

The next version should intentionally reduce or delay the following:

- generic SaaS section stacking
- card-heavy storytelling
- overexposure of AI engine selection too early
- changelog-style content in the main conversion path
- community and marketplace messaging before the core product is understood

These areas can still exist, but they should support the main narrative rather than compete with it.

## Reference Logic To Absorb

The reference direction from `realoficial.com.br` is useful for structure, not imitation.

The main lessons to absorb are:

- show the promise fast
- demonstrate the product visually and concretely
- explain the workflow in very few steps
- use examples and feature pages to deepen trust
- make onboarding obvious
- repeat the call to action after each major understanding point

Genfy should borrow that strategic clarity, while keeping its own product identity and commerce-specific differentiation.

## Ideal Narrative Structure

The landing page should tell a single coherent story.

Recommended narrative order:

1. Hero
2. Product proof
3. How it works
4. Demos and examples
5. Core capabilities
6. Strategic differentiation
7. Onboarding
8. Final conversion

### 1. Hero

The hero should answer four questions immediately:

- what is Genfy
- who is it for
- what outcome it produces
- what the user should do next

The hero should not feel abstract or overly technical. It should feel like a product with a direct business outcome.

### 2. Product Proof

Very early on, the page should prove the product is real.

The proof layer can be based on:

- example outputs
- product-to-video transformations
- example product flows
- supported use cases

This section exists to reduce doubt before the visitor reads deeper.

### 3. How It Works

The ideal explanation is short and operational.

A likely Genfy structure is:

1. bring in the product
2. validate or enrich the opportunity
3. generate the video and publish faster

The goal is not to explain every subsystem. The goal is to make the first mental model easy.

### 4. Demos and Examples

This is one of the most important upgrades.

The landing page should show concrete examples such as:

- upload a product image and get a selling video
- paste a TikTok Shop link and create from live product context
- find a trending product and move directly into creation

The demos should make the platform feel actionable, not theoretical.

### 5. Core Capabilities

Feature communication should be grouped by user value, not by internal product modules.

Good capability themes:

- discover products
- validate opportunities
- create faster
- choose output quality
- manage production flow

This keeps the page strategic instead of fragmented.

### 6. Strategic Differentiation

This section explains why Genfy matters.

It should frame Genfy against the manual alternative:

- searching products in one place
- validating in another
- creating content in another
- testing slowly
- operating without feedback loops

Genfy’s advantage is that it compresses those steps into a tighter system.

### 7. Onboarding

Onboarding should become a visible part of the landing strategy.

The page should reduce uncertainty around the first action by making the first path explicit.

The onboarding idea should communicate:

- start with an image or link
- choose the simplest path first
- get a visible result quickly
- understand what happens next inside the app

This matters because a strong landing page is not only about persuasion. It is also about lowering activation friction.

### 8. Final Conversion

The final CTA should feel like the natural end of the story, not a repeated button with no added context.

By the end of the page, the visitor should already know:

- what Genfy does
- why it is useful
- how it works
- why it is better than the manual workflow
- how to begin

## Onboarding Theory

The landing page should function as pre-onboarding.

That means it should prepare the user for the first product interaction before the app opens.

Good onboarding outcomes for the page:

- the user understands the first input type
- the user understands what output to expect
- the user understands the time-to-value
- the user understands that Genfy is not only a generator, but a guided workflow

The strongest onboarding message is usually not “we have many features.”
It is “you can start this way, and you will get this result.”

## Content Principles

The next version should follow these content rules:

- one section, one responsibility
- fewer claims, more evidence
- less generic AI language
- more product-specific outcomes
- less repetition between sections
- shorter copy, clearer hierarchy

The page should read as if every line is trying to move the user toward understanding and action.

## Visual Direction Theory

The page should feel more editorial and product-led than decorative.

Desired qualities:

- strong first-screen composition
- visible product interface or believable output examples
- fewer generic glass cards
- more visual storytelling
- restrained motion with narrative purpose
- a more intentional hierarchy between brand, promise, proof, and action

The page should look premium, but it should not rely on style alone. The visual system should support comprehension.

## Role Of Supporting Pages

Not everything needs to live on the homepage.

The homepage should sell the core system.

Deeper pages can explain:

- individual tools
- AI engines
- feature breakdowns
- updates
- FAQs
- educational content

This separation is important because too much depth on the homepage weakens the conversion path.

## Success Criteria

The landing strategy is working if a new visitor can understand, within seconds:

- Genfy is for sellers and creators working around products
- Genfy helps find or validate what to create
- Genfy turns product inputs into publishable video outputs
- getting started looks simple and immediate

It is also working if the page feels more focused, more credible, and more memorable than a generic AI tool landing page.

## What This Document Does Not Decide

This document does not decide:

- final UI layout
- exact copy
- component structure
- animation implementation
- image assets
- technical architecture

Those decisions should come after a broader reference review and a product walkthrough using Playwright, especially once newer features are inspected.

## Next Input Needed Later

Before implementation, the next review cycle should gather:

- screenshots and notes from the current product experience
- newly shipped features worth promoting
- strongest real demo flows
- best onboarding entry point
- proof elements that are credible enough to show publicly

At that point, this theory can be converted into a concrete landing plan with sections, copy, visual references, and implementation priorities.
